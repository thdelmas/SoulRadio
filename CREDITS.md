- **Shika no Tōne (*Distant Cry of Deer*) — shakuhachi honkyoku**
  Performed by Araki Kodō III (1879–1935) on the shakuhachi (vertical bamboo flute), Victor 13029, recorded between 1925 and 1937. *Honkyoku* are the original Zen Buddhist solo pieces of the Fuke sect — breath-driven long tones used as meditation, not performance. The recording's denoised derivative was released to the public domain by its uploader (Membeth, 2008); the original is PD in Japan and PD-US-record.

- **Tibetan monks chanting — Gyuto Branch Monastery, McLeod Ganj**
  Field recording by Samuel Corwin (House No. 422, McLeod Ganj, Dharamshala — the Tibetan exile community in northern India). Gyuto monks are known for the deep overtone (multiphonic) chant tradition — the lowest-register sung sound on earth, and the closest acoustic equivalent to a sub-audible drone the night band is designed around.
